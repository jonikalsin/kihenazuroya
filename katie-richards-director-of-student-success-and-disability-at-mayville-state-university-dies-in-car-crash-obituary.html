Katie Richards, director of student success and disability at Mayville State University, dies in car

Hope, ND – The Mayville State University community is grappling with profound sadness following the devastating news of the sudden passing of Katie Richards, Director of Student Success and Disability Support Services. Katie, a renowned figure in the University of North Dakota’s Athletic Hall of Fame, was tragically involved in a fatal car accident early Thursday morning in Reynold.

The incident, which occurred around 12:10 A.M., unfolded as Katie’s southbound 2021 Buick Enclave veered off 22nd Avenue NE, approximately eight miles west of Reynolds in Traill County, and somersaulted multiple times. Despite the prompt response from law enforcement, Katie was pronounced deceased at the scene of the accident.

North Dakota Highway Patrol’s investigation revealed that Katie was the sole occupant of the vehicle at the time of the crash and was not utilizing her seatbelt. An accomplished athlete, Katie’s legacy extended back to her notable contributions to the 1998-99 Women’s Basketball. Her impact extended beyond her athletic career, as she served as an esteemed Director of Student Success and Disability Services at Mayville State University.

Furthermore, her remarkable coaching career at May-Port-CG High School since 2009 underscored her enduring commitment to the growth and development of young athletes. Previously recognized for her achievements in the sport, Katie’s exceptional talents were acknowledged with accolades such as the all-state basketball team at Hope High School and the honor of being named an honorable mention All-American during her tenure with the University of North Dakota Sioux.

Recently, the University of North Dakota Athletic Hall of Fame inducted Katie Richards and her fellow members from the 1998–1999 NCAA Women’s Basketball Division II Championship squad, solidifying her enduring impact on the world of athletics.

Further details concerning Katie Richards’ obituary and funeral arrangements will be communicated by her bereaved family.
