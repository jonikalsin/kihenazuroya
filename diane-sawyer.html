Diane Sawyer is a famous American television journalist. Diane Sawyer is famously known as the anchor of ABC News‘s nightly flagship program ABC World News. Moreover, she is also a co-anchor of ABC News’s morning news program Good Morning America and Primetime News magazine. Currently, she is a correspondent on 20/20 since 1998.

Early Life and Childhood

The full name of Diane Sawyer is Lila Diane Sawyer. She was born on the 22nd of December 1945. At present, she is 77 years old. She has a zodiac sign of Capricorn. Her birthplace is Glasgow, Kentucky. Her parents are Jean W. and Erbon Powers “Tom” Sawyer, a county judge. She has a sister named Linda Sawyer.

Furthermore, the reporter’s nationality is American and her ancestry includes English, Irish, Scots-Irish, and German. Her family shifted to Louisville after her birth. Sawyer’s father rose to local prominence as a Republican politician and community leader.

Education

Specifically, Diane Sawyer is a graduate student at Seneca High School in the Buechel area of Louisville. She was active in various artistic activities in high school. In addition, she also worked as an editor-in-chief for her school newspaper, The Arrow.

The capable newsreader accomplished a Bachelor of Arts with a major in English from Wellesley College in Wellesley, Massachusetts. Before turning to journalism, she attended one semester of law school at the University of Louisville. Likewise, she was a member of the Wellesley College Blue Notes.

Diane Sawyer – Professional Life

After completing her graduation, Sawyer went back to Kentucky. She also was employed as a weather forecaster for WLKY-TV in Louisville. Besides, she was also a teacher until 1970. She served as a White House press aide for the following four years. Similarly, she was also employed as a Literary Assistant to President Richard Nixon from 1974 to 1978.

After a while, she played the role of CBS reporter and correspondent (1978-1981). In 1981, the newsperson was the co-anchor in Morning with Charles Kuralt. From 1982 to 1984, she became the announcer of  CBS Early Morning News. The correspondent of 60 Minutes for two years, also was the co-anchor for Primetime Live and 20/20.

Correspondingly, Diane performed on Good Morning America as a co-anchor in January 1999 for 10 years. For six years, she treated herself as a reporter on PrimeTime Thursday (2000-2006).  She was a great announcer on ABC World News from 2009 to 2014.

Presently, she is well known as a special contributor to ABC World News since September 2014.

Awards and Nominations

Sawyer is among those journalists who have had a long list of awards throughout her career life. In 1997, she was inducted into the Television Hall of Fame. The outstanding journalist was the winner of the Daytime Emmy Awards for excellence in morning programming.

The talented woman was among one of the thirty most powerful women in America. This was considered by the Ladies Home Journal in 2001. Adding on, recognition as the winner of the Emmy Awards for outstanding news and documentary program achievement in 2007.

Equivalently, the ‘Peabody Award’, ‘The Robert F. Kennedy Journalism Award’, and the Walter Cronkite Award for Excellence in Journalism’ are some of the important awards of Diane. Also, she earned an honorary Doctorate of Letters from Brown University.

Recently, she achieved a Disney Legend, an award for making an outstanding contribution to the legacy of Walt Disney.
